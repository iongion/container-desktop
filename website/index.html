Consistent everywhere

One interface for every platform

Container Desktop looks and works the same on Windows, macOS and Linux — no mental remapping. It tells you exactly where it stores logs and settings, so you can always see what's happening behind the scenes.

  • Identical UI across all operating systems
  • Transparent about logs & configuration
  • Debug-friendly by design
Bring your own engine

Podman, Docker and Apple™ Container, your way

Connect to a native engine on Linux, a virtualized one anywhere, LIMA on macOS, or WSL on Windows — plus Apple™ Container on Apple silicon. Switch engines and connections from one manager.

Containers

Full control, no popups in your way

Know the origin and status of every container. Jump to logs, env vars, mounts, ports and live stats. Start, stop, restart, open a terminal, or reach exposed services right in your browser.

  • Live logs, stats & inspection
  • Built-in terminal console
  • Works the same for Podman, Docker & Apple™ Container
AI assistant

An assistant that drives your engines

Ask in plain language and a built-in, local-first AI inspects and operates your containers — it calls typed tools and renders the results as rich cards, and asks before anything that changes state. Bring your own model: LM Studio or llama.cpp on your machine, or OpenRouter, Anthropic and more in the cloud.

  • Local-first — your data stays on your machine
  • Typed tools rendered as cards, with approvals
  • Your model, your keys (stored in the OS keychain)
Settings

Yours to configure, top to bottom

Set the theme and monospace font, decide how it starts and sits in the system tray, pick your log level, and always see exactly where settings and logs are stored.

  • Theme & monospace font to taste
  • Startup, tray & update controls
  • Transparent storage & log paths
Build images

Build images without leaving the app

Author a Containerfile with live linting, set your tags, build args and target stage, then build on your native engine and watch a layered timeline — cache hits, misses and durations — before inspecting the finished image's layers.

  • Monaco editor with live Containerfile linting
  • Live build timeline with cache analysis
  • Dive-style layer inspector
